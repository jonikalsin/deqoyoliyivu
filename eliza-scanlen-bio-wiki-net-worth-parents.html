    Eliza's Role In The 'Little Women'

    While Eliza has several credits on her name, she got a wide range of attention through her role as Beth March in the 2019 movie, Little Women.

    The Sydney native starred alongside Emma Watson, Saoirse Ronan, Florence Pugh, and Laura Dern star as Jo March, Meg March, Amy March, and Marmee March, respectively.

    Eliza Scanlen alongside co-stars Emma Watson, Saoirse Ronan, and Florence Pugh in 'Little Women' (©: IMDB)

    The story of Little Women plots around Beth and her four sisters growing up in post-Civil War, Massachusetts.

    The four sisters (Jo, Meg, Beth, and Amy), who come of age in America in the aftermath of the civil war, stand together through the difficult and struggling time despite their varied nature.

    Little Women With A Bang Budget Of $40 Million; Its Box Office Records

    'Little Women' is a movie directed by Greta Gerwig and the eighth film adaptation of the 1868's novel 'Little Women' by Louisa May Alcott. Alcott's novel caught Greta Gerwig's attention since she was a child. Concerning the book, she mentioned; 

    "It was just a part of me.

    These girls felt like my sisters, and their memories felt like my memories."

    The cover page of 1868's novel 'Little Women' by Louisa May Alcott (©: Amazon)

    After Greta met the filmmaker Noah Baumbach on Sony's remake of Greenberg in 2010, she convinced the producer to make her a crack at the script. Eventually, the Sony Chief, Tom Rothman, gave her the directing responsibility of 'Little Women' and the movie got directed with a budget of $40 million.

    The movie, which traced the story of four sisters growing up in post-Civil War Massachusetts first made its world premiere on the 7th of December 2019 at the Museum of Modern Art in New York City. Later, it was released by Sony Pictures Releasing on 25 December 2019 in the United States on the occasion of Christmas.

    After the release of the movie, Sony Pictures projected to gross between $16 million to $17 million for the 5-day Christmas holiday. As per the reports of Fandango, 93% of 18+ moviegoers a couple of movies during the holiday, and 34% will see at least four films.

    As of now, it appeared to have derived nearly $24 million in the box office and is ranked third or fourth each day.
