    Who is Jason Mraz?

    Jason Mraz is an American singer, songwriter known for his hit singles like The Remedy (I Won’t Worry), I Won’t Give Up and Make It Mine. Furthermore, he has two Grammy awards and many other nominations for his contribution to music.

    Age, Parents, Siblings, Family. Ethnicity, Education

    Jason Mraz was born on June 13, 1977, in Mechanicsville, Virginia, USA. His father, Tom Mraz, and mother,  June Tomes divorced when he was five years old.

    He was educated at Lee-Davis High School where he was a part of the cheerleading group, chorus, and drama club. After graduating from school in 1995, he went to American Musical and Dramatic Academy, and Longwood University.

    Mraz is of American nationality and of Czech and Slovak descent through his grandfather.
